Dangers Of Internet

The Internet is an incredible resource but also full of hidden risks that aren’t always easy to spot. The spread of misinformation is one of the biggest dangers that plague our society. With social media, false information can spread to millions of people in a matter of seconds. These platforms have the power to shape public opinion, influence elections, and even dictate trends. A manipulated image on Instagram, a conspiracy theory on X, or fake news on TikTok is quickly perceived as factual without regard to the severe consequences they have on society’s opinions.
These platforms prioritize engagement, so a sensational story or shocking headlines that get the attention of viewers can allow a creator to profit immensely, making it tempting to create false media for clicks and views. Unfortunately, most people using social media don’t take the time to fact-check what they are viewing, making the spread of fake news more dangerous than ever. This Internet dilemma has the potential to provoke division and fear just from one simple look at your social media feed, making it an emotional toll as well as an intellectual one.
Tech giants, like Mark Zuckerburg and Elon Musk, that own these platforms hold enormous power over the information that we see. The algorithms they use to decide which posts are presented on our feeds, create a curated reality of your personal life. And while there are advantages to having a personalized algorithm, the control that these individuals have to influence our thinking is too quickly overlooked. Unfortunately, these tech giants go unscathed without any responsibility for the dangers of their platforms.
With these platforms collecting so much of our personal data, cybersecurity has become a critical issue. From private messages to location data, hackers often target these platforms to access this personal information from its users. A single breach could expose millions of innocent people to fraud and identity theft.

Pedophilia & Cat-fish

One of the most concerning dangers of social media platforms is the exploitation of predators and catfishers, particularly targeting vulnerable populations like children and teenagers. These platforms have become hunting grounds for pedophiles who exploit the anonymity that these spaces provide. They can easily create fake profiles, posing as anyone they want to be and appearing trusting to young adults. Catfishing is another increasing issue that has taken over the social media space. People can easily create fake profiles, often used for financial spamming, emotional manipulation, or grooming. While there have been efforts to minimize this dilemma from the tech giants by implementing age restrictions, these efforts are oftentimes useless and easily passed. With the large scale of social media, it would be near impossible to stop every instance of this abuse. However, it is also clear that this alarming issue is not of serious concern to these billionaires that have control over the platforms.
