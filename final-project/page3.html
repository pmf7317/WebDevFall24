How to Fight the Bots

1. Delete All Social Media Accounts
Yes, all of them. Twitter? Gone. Facebook? Deactivated. Instagram? Never heard of it. Stop feeding the algorithmic monsters with your data. Social media platforms are simply digital cages where you’re the product. Just leave—before they know more about you than your therapist does.
2. Unplug from the Internet Forever
Remember when life didn’t revolve around Wi-Fi? Go back to that. Forget the cloud, turn off your router, and go full offline. No more digital surveillance. No more ads. Just you, the stars, and your own thoughts—before they get monetized.
3. Shun All “Smart” Devices
Your phone, your TV, your microwave, your fridge—throw them all out. They’re listening, watching, and most definitely tracking you. If it's connected to the internet, it’s a tool for surveillance. Embrace a flip phone. Embrace paper maps. And yes, maybe even embrace actual books.
4. Take a Year-Long Digital Detox
But not just from social media—everything. Delete your emails, uninstall all apps, and make sure your digital footprint is as invisible as possible. Only communicate via hand-written letters or smoke signals. (Bonus points for speaking in person, face-to-face.)
5. Communicate Exclusively in Code
Encrypted messages? Too mainstream. If you want to keep your thoughts safe from bots, start speaking in Morse code or binary. A bot will never understand you if it’s too busy trying to decipher your message. Bonus: You’ll sound mysterious.
6. Create Your Own Internet—No Bots Allowed
Buy a plot of land, start your own data server, and build a personal, decentralized network where you control the content and no one is spying on you. Why rely on Big Tech when you can own your own tiny internet? (Just be careful not to accidentally connect it to the world wide web. Small detail.)
7. Destroy All Data
Data is the new gold, and you've been mined. Back up everything to old-school floppy disks or tape drives. Then, destroy your digital devices. Shred every document. Erase every trace. No one should have access to your data. Even you. (You might need a new identity after this.)
8. Boycott All Automated Services
Voice assistants? Never heard of them. Chatbots? Don’t answer their calls. Refuse to interact with any AI, whether it’s answering questions or setting your thermostat. Humans should be the ones doing the work—not some machine that’s just trying to learn your patterns for profit.
9. Live Off the Grid and Outside the System
Literally. Forget the “cloud”—go live in an actual cloud forest (or a cave, your choice). No GPS, no cell towers, no AI assistants. Just you, nature, and the purest form of privacy possible. It's the only way to truly escape the omnipresent bots and their data-mining ways.
10. Only Use Technology from the Pre-Internet Age
Why settle for a robot vacuum when you could have a human maid, or better yet, sweep your own floors? Go old school with your tech choices—manual typewriters, landline phones, mechanical watches. Even better, learn to live without technology at all. The past is the new future.
11. Wear a Mask and Speak in Parables
Always wear a mask when interacting with the digital world. Create a persona so encrypted even the most sophisticated AI can’t decode it. Talk only in metaphors and parables so the bots can never quite figure out what you’re saying. Your thoughts are now safe... or are they?
12. Boycott All Smart Algorithms — Start Using Your Own Brain
This one’s simple: Instead of relying on recommendation engines and endless personalization algorithms, just use your own brain. Make decisions based on your own experiences, not what an algorithm tells you you should like. Don't let the bots dictate your life choices—especially what TV shows you watch.
13. Build a Fortress of Solitude
Construct a secret, impregnable fortress with walls so thick that no signal—whether 4G, 5G, or AI—can penetrate. Make sure to stock up on books, knowledge, and hand-made crafts. Bonus: Build a moat filled with giant firewalls.
